A networked upgrade of the original hot-seat rules. Blue hosts, Red joins, both exchange WebRTC session text once, then take turns on separate devices over a direct peer connection.

Online Session

Host creates the room as Blue. Join copies the host offer, creates an answer, and pastes it back. After the data channel opens, turns sync online automatically.

Before the peer link opens, the board stays read-only.

Match Controls

This version keeps the original ruleset, but each side now clicks from a separate browser. Reset and board size changes sync from the host to the guest.

Rules

Blue and Red alternate turns. In online mode, only the connected player whose turn it is may place a token.
You may only place on an empty hex or one you already control, exactly like the original local game.
When tokens exceed a cell's neighbor count, it explodes outward, converts adjacent cells, and keeps chaining until the board stabilizes.

Blue Wins

The losing player has no tokens remaining on the board.
